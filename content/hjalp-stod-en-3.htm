Children are very welcome at the shelter!

Do not feel guilty. You have taken your children from a bad situation. A situation that was neither your or their fault that they were in. Even if it feels difficult, try to remember that it is better for them to live at the shelter than being scared. The children that suffers most is the once who live with violence and tensions at home.

Help your children. It is normal for children to feel confused about what is happening. Encourage them to ask questions if it is something they want to know. The women at the shelter will help you if you are too upset or have a hard time finding the right words. Make sure your children know that you can stay at the womenâ€™s shelter as long as you need. Some children are afraid that they will have to move back home.

Make yourself at home. Some children gets worried, clingy and sad when they arrive. Most often this will pass quickly so try not to panic. After a short period of time your children will enjoy themselves, their new home and their friends.

If it is possible we recommend you to let your children go to the same school or kindergarten. If you are afraid that your partner will contact your children and might abduct them it is important that you keep them at home.


Common questions

Which women get beaten?

There is not a special type of women. Anyone can be the victim. It does not matter how old you are, where you live, where you are from or what you work with.

Which men beat women?

There is not a special type of man either. It can be anyone. Many men does not see it as abuse when they beat their partner. They just want to be in control and show who is in power. They know they are wrong, otherwise they would hit women in front of people as well. They have a choice, and if they abuse they commit a crime.

Why does she not leave?

If you live with abuse you will loose your self-esteem. After a while they feel helpless without the men and makes sure no one knows about what is happening. No one can know they are getting beaten. They often take the blame for it and keep pretending everything is fine for years.

How often does women report abuse?'

Women demand their rights. It is a crime to beat and abuse. More women stands up for these rights and report their abusing partners. 17.932 women over 18 reported abuse in 1994. In 2013 the number of reports increased to 27.056. (source SCB). Even if it is an improvement, it is still a lot of women that are too afraid to do something about their situation.
